## ðŸŽ­ The Design Metaphor: "The World is a Stage"

Celoris rejects the traditional desktop metaphors of "Office" or "Workbench." Instead, it treats the computer as a **Theater**, and the software as a **Performance**.

### The Stage (Desktop Area)
*   **Meaning:** The center of attention. The place where the magic happens.
*   **Function:** Unlike a cluttered desk, the Stage is meant to be clean, highlighting only the active performers (Windows).

### The Backstage (Dock / Taskbar)
*   **Meaning:** The preparation area.
*   **Function:** Apps sitting here are not "closed"; they are "waiting in the wings," ready to step onto the stage at a moment's notice.

### The Curtain (App Launcher)
*   **Meaning:** The reveal.
*   **Function:** Opening the launcher is like drawing back the curtain to reveal the full cast of characters (Applications) available to the producer.

### The Spotlight (Window Manager)
*   **Meaning:** Focus and Attention.
*   **Function:** The active window is "under the spotlight." In future versions, this may be visually represented by subtle lighting effects, dimming the background to ensure the user's focus is on the star of the show.
